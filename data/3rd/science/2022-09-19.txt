Behavior: All of the actions and reactions of a plant or animal
Camouflage: The coloring or patterns on an animals body that allow it to blend in with its environment
Carnivore: An animal that eats only other animals
Community: A group of different populations that live together and interact in an environment
Ecosystem: All the living and nonliving things in an area that interact with each other
Environment: All the living and nonliving things that surround an organism includes things such as water, air, soil, weather, and landforms

Organism: any living thing
Population: all the organisms of one kind in an ecosystem
Predator: an animal that hunts and eats other animals for food

Producer: An organism that makes its own food (plants)
Survival: using adaptations to continue to live
Desert: An extremely dry habitat with little water and few plants
Wetlands: a lowland area, such as a marsh or swamp, that is saturated with moisture, especially when regarded as the natural habitat of wildlife.
Forest: a large area of land covered with trees
Ocean: A large body of salt water
Arctic: the area around the North Pole. Covered in Ice and Snow
Grassland: a large open area of country covered with grass, especially one used for grazing. In Africa it is called the savannahs
Rain Forest: Dense forest that receives high amounts of rain each year. Also known as the jungle.
