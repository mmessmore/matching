Adaptations: A change by which a species becomes better suited to its environment.
Biodiversity: The variety of life in a particular ecosystem.
Competition: The interaction that exists when organisms are needing the same resource.
Consumers: Organisms that eat other organisms and are classified by the organisms they eat for food (herbivore, carnivore, omnivore).
Decomposers: Organisms that break down other organisms and return nutrients to the environment.
Ecosystem: A community of organisms and their abiotic environment.
Population: A group of individuals of the same species that live in the same place at the same time.
Producers: Organisms that make their own food by receiving energy directly from the fun (photosynthesis).
Symbiotic: The relationship between organisms in which one depends on the other.  Some examples are mutualism, commensalism, and parasitism.
Scavengers: Organisms that eat organisms that are already dead.
